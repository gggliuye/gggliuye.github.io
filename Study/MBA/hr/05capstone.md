## 1. Internal and External Context

External Influences :
* Competition and Consumers
* Labor Markets and Demographics
* Legal and Social Pressure
* Technology

Internal Influences :
* Organizational Strategy guides HR Strategy
  * Managers need to align their team's efforts with the organization's objectives, whether in the private or nonprofit sector.
* Employee Motivation
  * Barriers to Full Work Effort : Different perceptions of work (e.g., as a curse, source of income, or fulfillment) can hinder an employee's ability to give full effort.
  * Implications for Work Motivation : Workers who view work as a source of rights may need minimum standards and a voice to enhance motivation.
  * Enhancing Worker Engagement :
    * Organizations should aim to shift perceptions of work towards fulfillment and identity to foster engagement.
    * Engagement can be driven through intrinsic rewards, social networks, and opportunities for service, depending on how employees view their work.


## 2. Recruiting and Selecting Employees
<p></p>

* Internal Recruiting : Promoting from within is less expensive and motivates current employees by providing growth opportunities.
  *  can create a ripple effect, requiring backfilling of positions and potential workplace politics.
* External Recruiting : Hiring externally brings in new ideas and diverse perspectives that can challenge existing practices.
  * may be unproven and costly, and relying too heavily on them can negatively impact employee morale and retention.
