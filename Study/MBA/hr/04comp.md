**Benchmarking Pay** (like buying a house): serves as a midpoint for salaries, with flexibility for a pay range based on market conditions.
1. The first step is to identify the job description, which guides the search for comparable external benchmarks.
2. The second step involves researching external job descriptions and their corresponding pay rates using resources like the Bureau of Labor Statistics and various salary websites.
3. Make adjustments.

**Surveys and the Labor Market.**
A compensation survey aims to compare how different employers pay for similar jobs, considering industry, geography, and job roles.
Understanding labor markets helps organizations adjust pay based on supply and demand dynamics, which are represented graphically with wage on the y-axis and quantity of labor on the x-axis.

**Salary Structure:** using **Broadbanding**
(1) Job Titles: Ranging from Junior Engineers to Senior Engineers, indicating levels of responsibility.
(2) Pay Ranges: Each job title has a minimum and maximum salary, with promotions required for higher pay.

## 3. Benefits and Compliance

**Legal Framework** Federal laws set minimum wage standards, States can establish higher minimum wages.

* Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) : Covers minimum wage, overtime, child labor, and nondiscrimination.
* Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) : Requires 12 weeks of unpaid job-protected leave for medical emergencies, childbirth, and adoption.
* Other Key Statutes
  * OASDI (Old-Age, Survivors, and Disabilities Insurance)
  * ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)
* Health Insurance and Labor Relations


**Benefits** are a key component of the pay mix, which includes base pay, short-term incentives, and long-term incentives.
* **Statutory benefits**.
* **Perks**(津贴) are non-statutory benefits, also known as fringe benefits or perquisites, that employers offer beyond the legal minimum.

**Health Insurance** : Deductibles, Co-Insurance, Co-Pay. **Affordable Care Act (ACA)** (Obamacare), aims to expand health coverage and control health costs.

**Pension**(养老金) plans supplement retirement income by contributing funds into a special retirement fund.
They offer tax advantages by deferring taxes until retirement when individuals are typically in lower tax brackets.

## 4. Non-monetary Rewards

**Total Rewards Model**.
* Non-monetary rewards are essential components.
* Non-monetary rewards are subjective.
* Value to Employers : Non-monetary rewards can reflect company values and culture, attracting employees who align with those values.
* Value to Employees : Research indicates that employees prioritize opportunities for learning and growth, flexibility, time off, company values, and promotion opportunities as key non-monetary rewards.
* Three common elements : <u>Recognition, Learn & Growth, Flexibility.</u>
