## 1. What Is Advertising? Definition & History

Definition:
* James Laver's Definition: **Advertising is any device which first arrests the attention  of the passer-by and then induces him to accept a mutually advantageous exchange.**
  * e.g. flower attracts butterfly using its color.
  * **Broadly**. This broad definition highlights the complexity and antiquity of advertising.
* Raymond Williams' Definition: **Advertising is the official art of capitalist society.**
  * **Narrowly**.Linking it to the social context of capitalism and emphasizing its role in promoting consumerism.
* John E. Kennedy's Definition: **Advertising is salesmanship in print**.
  * **In terms of media**.A salesman situation, Transforming face-to-face sales interactions into mass communication through various media.


Brief History:
* Early Advertising in America.
  * Began in the 1600s, influenced by English practices.
  * Benjamin Franklin, introduced illustrations in ads, and made them more visually appealing.
* Evolution in the 19th Century.
  * Manufacturing began to grow, leading to more domestic products being advertised.
  * James Gordon Bennett, treating ads as news.
* Advertising Agencies and Media Transformation.
  * The emergence of modern advertising agencies.
  * The introduction of radio and television.
* The rise of the Internet.

### 3.1. Semiotics and Advertising

**Semiotics** (Á¨¶Âè∑Â≠¶) is the study of systems of signs (signifier + signified). then **Advertising** is the study of Semiotics system.
* <u>Ad for Milk (sign) = Superman (signifier) + Milk (signified)</u>. In the ad, the quality of superman (signifier) and milk (signified) are linked (this process is called semiotics).
* Ads use signifiers to transfer attributes to commodities.

<u>Building Brands Through Contrast</u>. Brands are built by carefully selecting signifiers that convey specific attributes and values, often in opposition to other brands.

### 3.2. The intentions of ad creators and the interpretations of viewers

The distinction between the intentions of ad creators and the interpretations of viewers.

[Peter Pan Peanut Butter Commercial](https://youtu.be/GsBKYaeVJ2Y?si=BobZRZqcs7Fz6lWK). The commercial's ideological message suggests that consumption can bridge cultural differences, which is critiqued as a false narrative.

[1984 Apple commercial](https://youtu.be/VtvjbmoDx-I?si=lOTdbIMGbitRHZik) positions Apple as a liberating force against IBM's dominance, referencing Orwell's "1984" (Big Brother). In contrast, [IBM's commercial](https://youtu.be/mT-vb0yXC1o?si=mu2Aj9jwhUx14ZHQ) promotes productivity and harmony through technology, but critiques suggest it <u>misrepresents the reality of factory work.</u>

### 3.3. Ads and Art

The interpretation of art often involves questioning the <u>artist's intent and the societal context</u>, which can be challenging when the artist is unknown or the society has changed. Similar to art, advertisements are often interpreted without access to <u>the creators' intentions</u>, leading to varied personal meanings.

<u>It is dangerous to assume a literal representation from the eyes of the viewers.</u>
It is important to understand deeper meanings in both art and advertising, viewers should consider the context and cultural codes that shape interpretation.
